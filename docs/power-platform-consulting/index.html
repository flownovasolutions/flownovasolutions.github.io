Common Questions

Power Platform Consulting — Answered

What does a Power Platform consultant actually do?

A Power Platform consultant designs, builds, and deploys custom business solutions using Microsoft's low-code tools — Power Apps, Power Automate, Power BI, and Copilot Studio. Unlike a generalist IT consultant, we focus specifically on translating your operational problems into working systems: automated workflows, custom applications, AI agents, and dashboards that connect to your existing Microsoft 365 environment.

Do you work with businesses outside of Tulsa and Oklahoma?

Yes — FlowNova works with clients across the US. We're headquartered in Tulsa, Oklahoma, but Power Platform work is remote-first by design. Discovery calls, architecture sessions, and delivery all happen over Teams. Most of our clients never need us on-site.

What's the difference between Power Apps and Power Automate?

Power Apps is where you build the interface — the form, the dashboard, the tool your team interacts with. Power Automate is the engine running in the background — handling approvals, sending notifications, syncing data, and triggering actions. In most real-world solutions, they work together: Power Apps surfaces the experience, Power Automate does the heavy lifting behind it.

Do I need a large IT team to get value from Power Platform?

No. Most of FlowNova's clients are mid-sized businesses with lean IT teams or none at all. Power Platform operates within your existing Microsoft 365 licenses — no new infrastructure required. We handle the architecture and build, then document and train your team so they can own it going forward.

How is FlowNova different from a large Microsoft partner?

Large partners typically deploy teams of junior consultants managed by senior account leads you rarely talk to. At FlowNova, you work directly with the engineer building your solution — no handoffs, no middlemen. That means faster discovery, tighter scoping, and builds that actually reflect your real workflow rather than a templated assumption about it.
